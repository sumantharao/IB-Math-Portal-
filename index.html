Course Structure and Content

IB Mathematics students can select either Analysis and Approaches (AA) or Applications and Interpretation (AI), at Standard or Higher Level. AA courses adopt a more analytical approach, focusing on mathematical reasoning, rigorous argument, and proof, whereas AI courses emphasize contextual understanding and applying mathematics to real-life situations.

The courses are arranged into five broad topics: Number & Algebra; Functions; Geometry & Trigonometry; Statistics & Probability; and Calculus. Higher Level students study all Standard Level content in greater depth and explore additional HL-only topics, ensuring a more challenging and comprehensive understanding of mathematics.

At the end of the program, all SL and HL students sit the IB Mathematics examinations, with HL students additionally completing Paper 3, an extended problem-solving paper. Every student also completes an internal assessment (exploration), offering an opportunity to apply mathematics to a personally chosen topic.

Students are encouraged to practice the topics listed in this portal, reviewing lesson notes, attempting practice exercises, checking worked solutions, and watching the tutorial videos. This preparation ensures a solid foundation for the IB Mathematics course.
